Our Position

Why We're Signing

We, concerned students, formally call on our universities, national examination bodies, and relevant government authorities to phase out traditional high-stakes examinations and replace them with continuous, coursework-based assessments.

This is not a call to avoid accountability — it is a call for assessment methods that are humane, equitable, and fit for the challenges of the 21st century.

🧠 The Mental Health Crisis

Exam anxiety is a medically recognized condition affecting millions of students worldwide. High-stakes, single-sitting exams create extreme pressure that leads to burnout, depression, and in severe cases, self-harm. Continuous assessment distributes that pressure more humanely across the academic calendar.

🌿 The Environmental Cost

Mass examinations consume enormous quantities of paper, plastic packaging, and energy. Coursework-based systems reduce the material demands of assessment significantly, aligning education with climate responsibility.

📚 Exams Measure the Wrong Things

A 3-hour exam primarily tests memory and composure under pressure — not genuine understanding, creativity, or professional competence. Projects and portfolios paint a far more accurate picture of a student's abilities.

⚖️ The Equity Problem

Exams disproportionately disadvantage students with learning disabilities, test anxiety, and unstable home environments. Alternative assessments create a more level playing field for all learners.

We respectfully demand a formal review of examination policy, meaningful consultation with students, and a concrete roadmap toward coursework-based assessment systems.

The way we learn has changed. The way we are assessed must change too.
